About United India Foundation

United India Foundation (UIF) is a public charitable trust headquartered in Rishikesh, Uttarakhand, working across five pillars—Legal Aid, Environment, Education, Health, and Community Outreach—to build a nation that is fearless, aware, and sustainable. Our motto, “One Nation, One Future,” reflects a simple idea: when every Indian is empowered, India is united.

Our Mission

To empower individuals and communities with access to justice, quality learning, better health, a greener environment, and reliable social support—so that they can live with dignity, safety, and opportunity.

Our Vision

An India where rights are protected, voices are heard, resources are shared fairly, and communities thrive through people-powered action—from village wards to state capitals.

Our Values

Nyaya (Justice): Law must protect, not intimidate.
Seva (Service): People first—especially the underserved.
Sustainability: Long-term impact over short-term fixes.
Integrity: Transparent funds, clear processes, independent audits.
Unity in Diversity: Non-partisan, non-religious, and inclusive.

What We Do

Legal Aid & Justice Support

  • Free legal clinics, case triage, RTI help, and documentation support
  • Rights awareness sessions (women, senior citizens, workers, students)
  • Referral network of advocates and paralegals

Environment & Sustainability

  • Tree plantation & urban clean-up drives
  • Water & waste management awareness
  • Climate literacy workshops for schools & RWAs

Education & Youth Empowerment

  • After-school support and digital literacy labs
  • Mentorship & scholarships for deserving students
  • Life-skills & career guidance, especially for first-gen learners

Health & Wellness

  • Health camps, blood donation, and preventive screening
  • Mental health counselling & addiction support (with partners)
  • Yoga & wellness sessions for community resilience

Community Outreach & Relief

  • Women/child protection helpdesks, senior citizen care
  • Emergency relief & rehabilitation during disasters
  • Helpline, workshops, and local leadership programs

Who We Serve

  • Individuals facing misuse of law, domestic/legal harassment, or procedural barriers
  • Women, children, and senior citizens needing protection and support
  • Low-income families seeking education/health assistance
  • Communities vulnerable to climate and disaster risks
